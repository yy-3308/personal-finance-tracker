About

Why this exists

The Problem

Every personal finance app I tried had the same problems:

🚫
Not built for me
Most apps are either too bloated or too opinionated about how I should budget. And none of them support equity compensation, HSA investments, or multi-institution imports in the formats I actually get.
🔒
They want all my data
Linking bank credentials to third-party services means someone else has access to my full transaction history. One breach and it's all exposed. And there's no telling how these apps might use or monetize that data down the road.
💸
Too expensive for what they do
Mint shut down. YNAB is $100/year. Copilot is $70/year. I just want to see my spending and net worth — I shouldn't need a subscription for that.

The Solution

So I built my own. It runs 100% locally on your machine, reads the bank statements you already download, and does exactly what you need — nothing more.

🏠
Runs Locally
Your data never leaves your computer

What's Next
